The band

Radiohead is one of the most influential and critically acclaimed bands in modern music. Known for their experimental approach and genre-defying sound, the band has consistently pushed the boundaries of rock, electronic, and alternative music since their formation.
The members are:

  • Thom Yorke (singer)
  • Phil Selway (drums)
  • Johnny Greenwood (lead guitar)
  • Colin Greenwood (bass)
  • Ed O'Brien (guitar)

History

Radiohead was formed in the mid-1980s at Abingdon School (a private boys' school) in Abingdon-on-Thames, Oxfordshire, England, initially as a covers band.
They started playing in the school's music room, taking their name from the only day of the week they could rehearse: On a Friday.
The band performed their first live show at the Jericho Tavern in Oxford in late 1986.
The band changed their name, drawing inspiration from a song by Talking Heads from the album True Stories (1986) called "Radio Head."
